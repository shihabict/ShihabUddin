About

I’m a PhD researcher in Computer Engineering at the University of Alabama in Huntsville, specializing in reinforcement learning, intelligent transportation systems, and natural language processing. With 4+ years of industry experience as an AI/NLP engineer, I’ve led projects ranging from Bangla language model development and ASR optimization to vector-based retrieval systems using large language models (LLMs) and ChromaDB. My early work focused on building practical NLP solutions — including a BERT-powered resume extractor, job recommendation engines, and a custom Bangla sentence transformer. In my current research, I’m applying deep reinforcement learning to real-world mobility challenges like car-following modeling and traffic control. I’m also exploring how LLMs can be used to make transportation systems more context-aware, such as summarizing incident reports or extracting actionable insights from unstructured traffic data. I enjoy working at the intersection of applied AI and real-world systems, and I’m always open to collaboration — whether in research, open source, or industry R&D.
